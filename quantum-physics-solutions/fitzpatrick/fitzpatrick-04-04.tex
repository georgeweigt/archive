\fbox{\parbox{\dimexpr\linewidth-2\fboxsep-2\fboxrule}{
4-4. Demonstrate that if a particle in a one-dimensional
stationary state is bound then the expectation value of
its momentum must be zero.
}}

\bigskip
FIXME

\bigskip
For distance $d$ and time $t$ the expected velocity $\langle v\rangle$ is
\begin{equation*}
\langle v\rangle=\lim_{t\rightarrow\infty}\frac{d}{t}=0
\end{equation*}

Hence the expected momentum is also zero.
